01 · What we saw on the current site

The foundation is good. The story isn't landing.

Millennials4Boards has the proposition, the people, and the proof points. The current site is generic where it should be specific — it tells visitors what the network is, but rarely shows them what it actually does, who is behind it, or why anyone has written about it.

01 · Proof missing

Strong stats, no sources.

"57" and "75%" land hard — but without attribution, sceptical readers discount them. The current site asserts. The new site cites.

Current: "57 the average age of new Board members in Europe" — unsourced.
Proposed: 57 yrs · Egon Zehnder, 2024.

02 · One CTA, three audiences

"Join Us!" asks too much, too soon.

Aspiring directors, sitting directors, and corporates arrive on the same page with the same single ask. Each needs a different conversation.

Proposed: three paths on the home page — Aspiring · Sitting · Corporate.

03 · Vision before proof

Mission statements sit where testimonials should.

"Vibrant community" and "dynamic mentor-mentor program" are abstractions. Boardroom buyers want to see names, seats landed, press clippings.

Proposed: named testimonials, a press pullquote strip, and a journal rail with real titles.

04 · Team without names

Board carousel, no identities.

A rotating photo strip with no labels hides the network's best asset. Who these people are is the offer.

Proposed: uniform B&W portraits, named cards, role, sector, LinkedIn — in a varied masonry grid.

05 · Commercial path buried

Memberships page doesn't close.

Tiered pricing exists, but there's no structured checkout, no trust cues, and no transparent VAT/total. For a CHF 300+ purchase, decision hygiene matters.

Proposed: two pricing cards side-by-side, then a three-step wizard with a sticky order summary.

06 · Look and feel, 2018

The brand has moved on. The site hasn't.

Flat top bar, mixed weights, stocky photography. The new identity system (Poppins, Mint Leaf, editorial tone) never makes it to the live site.

Proposed: floating pill nav, full-bleed typographic hero, B&W portraits, mint-only accent.

02 · Five rules we held to

Every decision earns its place.

If a section didn't land against one of the five, we cut it. The result is a site that does less, but says more.

01

Specific over generic.

Sources on every stat. Named testimonials. Real article titles.

02

Three audiences, three doors.

Aspiring · Sitting · Corporate — each routed in under five seconds.

03

Proof before pitch.

Press, testimonials, and member wins appear early — before any mission statement.

04

Brand-strict.

Poppins Light + SemiBold only. Mint Leaf as the single accent.

05

Commercial clarity.

Memberships → wizard → Stripe. Transparent VAT. Trust at the decision moment.

03 · The navigation, in one picture

A small change, outsized effect.

A floating pill with the logo at the centre and the CTA alongside. Reads as premium, modern, and deliberately different from the sea of flat top bars.

Today
Home · Team · About us · Memberships · News and events · Partnerships · Contact us

Flat top bar, seven links, one CTA.

Functional but anonymous. Visually indistinguishable from a thousand other sites. "Join Us!" asks one question of three very different audiences.

Proposed
About · Team · Memberships · · Partnerships · Journal · Contact

Floating pill, logo-centred, CTA alongside.

Six links split around the mark. "Journal" replaces "News and events." "Join the network" lives alongside the pill — so the mark and the act of joining are visually separated.
